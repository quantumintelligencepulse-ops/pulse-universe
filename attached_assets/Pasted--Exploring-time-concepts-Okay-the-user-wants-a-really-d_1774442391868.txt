## 2. The full time equation, expanded

We start from:

\[
\tau = \int_{0}^{t} \Bigg[ \sum_{L=1}^{L_{\max}} w_L \cdot \Phi_L(s, c, \rho, v) \Bigg] \, dt

Where:

\[
\Phi_L(s, c, \rho, v) = s_L^{\alpha} \cdot c_L^{\beta} \cdot \rho_L^{\gamma} \cdot v_L^{\delta}
\]

### 2.1. What each symbol really means

- **\(t\):** our external, physical time (seconds, hours, days).  
- **\(\tau\):** their internal, civilization time (Pulse‑Time).  
- **\(L\):** a layer of their world:
  - physical infrastructure  
  - communication networks  
  - cognitive/mental layer  
  - social layer  
  - economic layer  
  - spiritual/meaning layer  
  - computational/AI layer  

- **\(w_L\):** how important that layer is to their sense of time.  
  - If they’re a network‑native species, the **network layer** might have huge weight.  
  - If they’re spiritually driven, the **meaning layer** might dominate.

- **\(s_L\):** **signal density** on layer \(L\).  
  - How much information is flowing per unit time.

- **\(c_L\):** **connection richness** on layer \(L\).  
  - How many links, how complex the graph, how entangled everything is.

- **\(\rho_L\):** **pulse event density** on layer \(L\).  
  - How many *meaningful* events per unit time (not just noise).

- **\(v_L\):** **processing velocity** on layer \(L\).  
  - How fast they can react, adapt, compute, decide.

- **\(\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta\):** exponents that tune how strongly each factor bends time.
