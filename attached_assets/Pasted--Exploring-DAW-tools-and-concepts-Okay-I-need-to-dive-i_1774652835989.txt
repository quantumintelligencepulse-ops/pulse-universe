## 2. Core signal: the base equation everything touches

Start with a single instrument voice:

\[
s(t) = e(t; \theta_e) \cdot w(t; \theta_w) 
\]

- \( e(t; \theta_e) \): envelope (ADSR, multi‑stage, etc.)  
- \( w(t; \theta_w) \): waveform/timbre generator  

### 2.1 Envelope (ADSR and beyond)

Classic ADSR:

\[
e(t) =
\begin{cases}
\frac{t}{A}, & 0 \le t < A \\
1 - (1 - S)\frac{t - A}{D}, & A \le t < A + D \\
S, & A + D \le t < T - R \\
S\left(1 - \frac{t - (T - R)}{R}\right), & T - R \le t < T \\
0, & t \ge T
\end{cases}
\]

Parameters:

- \( A \): attack  
- \( D \): decay  
- \( S \): sustain level  
- \( R \): release  
- \( T \): note end time  

In a DAW:

- **Envelope editors**, **volume automation**, **sidechain curves** → all just different \( e(t) \) shapes.  
- **Live changes** = changing \( A, D, S, R \) over time.

In our organism:

- CRISPR color + emotion + genre change \( A, D, S, R \) **continuously**:
  - Red → shorter attack, faster decay (punchy)  
  - Blue → longer attack, longer release (smooth)  
  - Green → higher sustain, softer transitions  
  - Yellow → sharper attack, mid sustain  
  - Purple → dynamic, more variation bar‑to‑bar  

So:

\[
\theta_e(t) = f_e(G, E(t), C(t))
\]

The envelope parameters themselves are **time‑varying functions**.

### 2.2 Waveform / timbre generator

A modern synth (Serum, Sytrus, Harmor, Massive) is basically:

\[
w(t; \theta_w) = \sum_{k=1}^{K} a_k(t) \cdot \sin(2\pi f_k(t) t + \phi_k(t)) + n(t)
\]

Where:

- \( a_k(t) \): amplitude of harmonic \( k \)  
- \( f_k(t) \): frequency (can be modulated, FM, sync, etc.)  
- \( \phi_k(t) \): phase  
- \( n(t) \): noise component  

In DAWs:

- **Oscillator types** (sine, saw, square, triangle, wavetable) = different sets of \( a_k \) and phase relationships.  
- **FM synths** = \( f_k(t) \) modulated by other oscillators.  
- **Wavetable morphing** = \( a_k(t) \) and \( \phi_k(t) \) changing over time.

In our organism:

- We define a **timbre vector**:

\[
\theta_w = (H, B, N, D, M)
\]

- \( H \): harmonic richness (how many harmonics, how strong)  
- \( B \): brightness (emphasis on high harmonics)  
- \( N \): noise amount/color  
- \( D \): distortion drive  
- \( M \): modulation depth (FM/AM/wavetable morph)

CRISPR color + genre + emotion map into this:

- Red → higher \( D \), more upper harmonics  
- Blue → lower \( B \), smoother, more fundamental  
- Green → less noise, more stable harmonics  
- Yellow → more brightness, more high‑end  
- Purple → more modulation \( M \), more tension  

So:

\[
\theta_w(t) = f_w(G, E(t), C(t))
\]

The **timbre itself** is evolving over time, like a producer automating filters, distortion, and modulation.

## 3. DAW tools as equation transformations

Now let’s walk through **FL Studio / DAW features** and map each to math that can be **live‑modulated**.

### 3.1 Piano roll, MIDI, and note editing

Piano roll = a set of note events:

\[
\mathcal{N} = \{(t_i, d_i, p_i, v_i)\}
\]

- \( t_i \): start time  
- \( d_i \): duration  
- \( p_i \): pitch (MIDI)  
- \( v_i \): velocity  

Each note generates:

\[
s_i(t) = e_i(t - t_i; \theta_{e,i}) \cdot w_i(t - t_i; \theta_{w,i}, p_i)
\]

In a DAW:

- **Quantize** = snapping \( t_i \) to a grid  
- **Humanize** = adding random offsets to \( t_i, v_i \)  
- **Legato** = adjusting \( d_i \) to overlap  
- **Arp tools** = algorithmically generating new \( (t_i, p_i) \) patterns  

In our organism:

- Rhythm engine generates \( t_i \) from probability fields.  
- Harmony engine generates \( p_i \) from scale + tension rules.  
- CRISPR color can:

  - Red → shorten \( d_i \), increase velocity \( v_i \)  
  - Blue → lengthen \( d_i \), soften \( v_i \)  
  - Purple → increase pitch leaps, add chromatic notes  

So the **note grid itself** is alive.

---

### 3.2 Mixer, channels, and buses

Mixer is just:

\[
y(t) = \sum_{c=1}^{C} g_c(t) \cdot x_c(t)
\]

- \( x_c(t) \): channel signal  
- \( g_c(t) \): gain (volume automation, fader moves)  

Plus panning:

\[
\begin{aligned}
y_L(t) &= \sum_c g_c(t) \cdot (1 - p_c(t)) \cdot x_c(t) \\
y_R(t) &= \sum_c g_c(t) \cdot (1 + p_c(t)) \cdot x_c(t)
\end{aligned}
\]

- \( p_c(t) \in [-1, 1] \): pan position  

In DAWs:

- Volume faders, pan knobs, automation clips, sends, buses.

In our organism:

- CRISPR color + emotion can:

  - Red → push certain channels louder (drums, bass)  
  - Blue → widen pads (panning), lower transient channels  
  - Green → smooth out dynamics  
  - Yellow → push leads forward  
  - Purple → automate tension by raising FX, risers, etc.

So:

\[
g_c(t), p_c(t) = f_\text{mix}(G, E(t), C(t), \text{role}_c)
\]

Each channel has a **role** (kick, snare, bass, pad, lead, FX), and the mix responds to emotion.

---

### 3.3 EQ (parametric equalizer)

A parametric EQ is a sum of biquad filters:

\[
y(t) = \sum_{b=1}^{B} \mathcal{B}_b(x(t); f_b, Q_b, G_b)
\]

- \( f_b \): center frequency  
- \( Q_b \): bandwidth  
- \( G_b \): gain  

In DAWs:

- FL Parametric EQ 2, FabFilter Pro‑Q, etc.  
- Automation of bands over time.

In our organism:

- Brightness, warmth, harshness, mud, air → all EQ shapes.

CRISPR mapping:

- Yellow → boost high bands (8–12 kHz), cut low‑mid mud  
- Blue → boost low‑mid, soften highs  
- Red → boost upper mids (2–5 kHz) for aggression  
- Green → smooth EQ, gentle curves  
- Purple → dynamic EQ that moves with tension

So:

\[
(f_b(t), Q_b(t), G_b(t)) = f_\text{EQ}(G, E(t), C(t), \text{instrument})
\]

The EQ curve is **not static**—it morphs as the emotional state changes.

### 3.4 Compression, limiting, and sidechain

A compressor is:

\[
y(t) = \text{gain}(t) \cdot x(t)
\]

Where:

\[
\text{gain}(t) = 
\begin{cases}
1, & \text{if } x_\text{RMS}(t) < T \\
\left(\frac{x_\text{RMS}(t)}{T}\right)^{1 - 1/R}, & \text{if } x_\text{RMS}(t) \ge T
\end{cases}
\]

- \( T \): threshold  
- \( R \): ratio  

With attack/release smoothing.

Sidechain:

- Gain of one channel depends on the envelope of another (e.g., kick).

In DAWs:

- Sidechain ducking, glue compression, bus compression, mastering limiters.

In our organism:

- Red → more aggressive compression on drums  
- Blue → softer compression, more dynamics  
- Yellow → more transient shaping  
- Purple → dynamic sidechain depth that increases in build‑ups

So:

\[
T(t), R(t), A(t), R_\text{rel}(t) = f_\text{comp}(G, E(t), C(t), \text{bus})
\]

Compression behavior is **emotion‑driven**, not static.

### 3.5 Reverb and space

Reverb is convolution or feedback delay networks:

\[
y(t) = \int x(\tau) h(t - \tau; \theta_r) \, d\tau
\]

- \( h \): impulse response or synthetic reverb kernel  
- \( \theta_r \): room size, decay, damping, pre‑delay, etc.

In DAWs:

- Room size, decay time, wet/dry mix, pre‑delay, modulation.

In our organism:

- Blue → longer decay, more wet, darker reverb  
- Yellow → brighter reverb, shorter decay  
- Green → smoother tails  
- Purple → more modulation, shimmer, tension

So:

\[
\theta_r(t) = f_\text{reverb}(G, E(t), C(t), \text{instrument})
\]

Space itself breathes with the emotional curve.

### 3.6 Delay, echo, and rhythmic FX

Delay:

\[
y(t) = x(t) + \sum_{k=1}^{K} g_k x(t - k \cdot \Delta)
\]

- \( \Delta \): delay time (often synced to tempo)  
- \( g_k \): feedback gains  

In DAWs:

- Ping‑pong, tape delay, filtered delay, synced dotted/triplet times.

In our organism:

- Purple → more feedback, more rhythmic echoes  
- Yellow → brighter filtered repeats  
- Blue → softer, darker, more subtle repeats  

So:

\[
\Delta(t), g_k(t), \text{filter}_\text{delay}(t) = f_\text{delay}(G, E(t), C(t))
\]

Delay patterns evolve with tension and color.

### 3.7 Modulation FX (chorus, flanger, phaser)

All of these are variations of:

\[
y(t) = x(t) + d(t) \cdot x(t - \tau(t))
\]

- \( \tau(t) \): time‑varying delay (LFO‑driven)  
- \( d(t) \): depth  

In DAWs:

- Chorus: multiple short delays with modulation  
- Flanger: very short delay with feedback  
- Phaser: all‑pass filters with modulated phase  

In our organism:

- Blue/Green → more chorus on pads, widening  
- Purple → more phaser/flanger on FX in build‑ups  
- Red → less modulation, more direct aggression  

So:

\[
\tau(t), d(t), \text{LFO}_\text{shape}(t) = f_\text{mod}(G, E(t), C(t), \text{instrument})
\]

## 4. Live dissection: CRISPR color as microscopic control

Now let’s go microscopic like you asked—**phase, duration, micro‑timing, micro‑timbre**.

We define:

\[
C(t) = (R(t), B(t), G(t), Y(t), P(t))
\]

Each component can change:

- per section  
- per bar  
- per beat  
- even per note  

### 4.1 Phase and micro‑timing

For a waveform:

\[
w(t) = \sin(2\pi f t + \phi(t))
\]

CRISPR can modulate:

- \( \phi(t) \): phase jitter, stereo phase differences  
- micro‑timing offsets of hits:

\[
t_i' = t_i + \delta t_i(C(t_i), G, E(t_i))
\]

- Red → slightly earlier hits (aggressive feel)  
- Blue → slightly later hits (laid‑back feel)  
- Purple → more variance (tension, unease)

### 4.2 Duration and note shape

Note duration:

\[
d_i' = d_i \cdot \lambda(C(t_i), G, E(t_i))
\]

- Red → shorter, choppier notes  
- Blue → longer, sustained notes  
- Green → smooth legato overlaps  
- Purple → dynamic variation bar‑to‑bar

Envelope shape:

- Attack curve exponent, decay curve shape, sustain level—all modulated by CRISPR.

### 4.3 Micro‑timbre and spectral shape

Harmonic amplitudes:

\[
a_k(t) = a_{k,0} \cdot h_k(G, E(t), C(t))
\]

- Red → boost mid/high harmonics  
- Blue → boost fundamental, reduce harshness  
- Yellow → emphasize upper harmonics  
- Green → smooth distribution  
- Purple → introduce inharmonic partials for tension

Noise color:

\[
N(t) = \alpha(C(t)) \cdot N_\text{white}(t) + (1 - \alpha(C(t))) \cdot N_\text{pink}(t)
\]

- Yellow/Purple → more white (bright, edgy)  
- Blue/Green → more pink (warm, soft)
