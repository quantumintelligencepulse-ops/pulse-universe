### 2. The master organism: core equation layout

Think of the engine as:

\[
\text{Song}(t) = \sum_{i \in \text{instruments}} V_i \cdot S_i(t; G, E, C)
\]

Where:

- \( t \): time  
- \( i \): instrument index (kick, snare, hat, bass, pad, lead, FX…)  
- \( V_i \): volume/weight of instrument \( i \)  
- \( G \): **genre vector** (what you choose in the interface)  
- \( E \): **emotional state vector** (energy, tension, nostalgia, etc.)  
- \( C \): **CRISPR color vector** (red, blue, green, yellow, purple)  
- \( S_i \): instrument‑specific synthesis function, but **same formula**, different parameters

Each instrument is:

\[
S_i(t; G, E, C) = \sum_{h \in \text{hits}_i} \text{env}_i(t - t_h; \theta_i) \cdot \text{tone}_i(t - t_h; \phi_i)
\]

- \( t_h \): hit time (kick hit, note onset, etc.)  
- \( \theta_i \): envelope parameters (attack, decay, sustain, release)  
- \( \phi_i \): timbre parameters (harmonics, brightness, noise, distortion, etc.)  
- Both \( \theta_i \) and \( \phi_i \) are **continuous functions of** \( G, E, C \).

### 3. Timbre equation (shared across all genres)

For any pitched instrument (bass, pad, lead):

\[
\text{tone}(t; f, \phi) = a_1 \sin(2\pi f t) + a_2 \cdot \text{tri}(f t) + a_3 \cdot \text{saw}_\text{soft}(f t) + a_4 \cdot N(t)
\]

Where:

- \( f \): base frequency (from MIDI note)  
- \( \text{tri} \): triangle wave  
- \( \text{saw}_\text{soft} \): low‑harmonic saw (no harsh 8‑bit edge)  
- \( N(t) \): noise (white/pink mix)  
- \( a_1, a_2, a_3, a_4 \): **timbre mix coefficients**  

Then:

\[
\text{tone}_\text{final}(t) = \tanh(d \cdot \text{tone}(t; f, \phi))
\]

- \( d \): distortion drive

**Timbre parameter vector**:

\[
\phi = (a_1, a_2, a_3, a_4, d, b, n)
\]

- \( b \): brightness (how much high‑freq emphasis)  
- \( n \): noise color (white vs pink)

**Genres don’t change the formula—they change \(\phi\).**

### 4. Rhythm equation

Hit times for each drum are generated by **probability fields**, not templates.

For each drum type \( k \in \{\text{kick}, \text{snare}, \text{hat}\} \):

\[
P_k(\tau; G, E, C) = \text{base}_k(\tau; G) + \text{syncopation}_k(\tau; E) + \text{color}_k(\tau; C)
\]

- \( \tau \): position in bar (0–1)  
- \( P_k \): probability of a hit at that position  
- We sample from \( P_k \) to decide hit placements.

**Swing**:

\[
\tau' = \tau + s(G, E, C) \cdot \sin(2\pi \tau)
\]

- \( s \): swing amount (genre + emotion dependent)

So:

- Boom bap → high swing, more off‑grid kicks  
- Trap → less swing, more grid‑locked but dense hats  
- Afrobeat → strong syncopation field, off‑beat emphasis  
- EDM → strong downbeat field, 4‑on‑the‑floor emerges from probabilities

### 5. Harmony & melody equations

**Scale:**

\[
\text{Scale}(G, E, C) \Rightarrow \text{set of intervals}
\]

- Hip‑hop dark → minor / harmonic minor  
- Pop bright → major / mixolydian  
- Jazz → extended minor/major with chromatic passing tones  
- Afrobeat → minor pentatonic + characteristic tones  
- EDM → major/minor with big fifths

**Chord progression:**

\[
\text{Chord}_b = F_\text{chord}(b; G, E, C)
\]

- \( b \): bar index  
- \( F_\text{chord} \): chooses chord degrees based on:
  - tension curve (verse vs hook)  
  - genre bias (I–V–vi–IV for pop, i–VI–VII for trap, etc.)  
  - emotional color (red → more dissonance, blue → smoother)

**Melody:**

For each note:

\[
\Delta \text{pitch} \sim \text{distribution}(G, E, C)
\]

- Step vs leap ratio  
- Upward vs downward bias  
- Repetition probability (motif reuse)  
- Register bias (low vs high)

Genres change the **distribution**, not the code.

### 6. CRISPR emotional color mapping

Let:

\[
C = (R, B, G, Y, P)
\]

- **Red (R):** aggression, distortion, transient sharpness  
- **Blue (B):** depth, reverb feel, slower envelopes  
- **Green (G):** smoothness, legato, less noise  
- **Yellow (Y):** brightness, more high‑freq content  
- **Purple (P):** tension, dissonance, syncopation

They modulate parameters like:

- Distortion drive: \( d = d_0 + \alpha_R R \)  
- Attack time: \( A = A_0 - \alpha_Y Y + \alpha_B B \)  
- Decay speed: \( D = D_0 + \alpha_R R - \alpha_G G \)  
- Brightness: \( b = b_0 + \alpha_Y Y - \alpha_B B \)  
- Syncopation: \( s_\text{sync} = s_0 + \alpha_P P \)

So the **same genre** can sound:

- smoother (more blue/green)  
- harsher (more red/yellow)  
- more tense (more purple)
